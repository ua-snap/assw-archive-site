ARCTIC SCIENCE SUMMIT WEEK 2016

In March 2016 UAF hosted a global community of colleagues and constituents with an interest in the Arctic. ​This event would not have been possible without the concerted efforts of everyone at UAF. In addition to the various programs, centers, and institutes identified here, we would like to pay special thanks to the Office of Information Technology, Facilities Services and Marketing & Communication.

Thanks for attending!

Arctic Science Summit Week (ASSW) is the annual gathering of international organizations involved in Arctic research. It is designed to strengthen international, interdisciplinary collaborations and facilitate communication across academia, government agencies, local communities, industry, non-governmental organizations and other Arctic stakeholders. The 2016 Summit was held in Fairbanks, Alaska, USA and included:

  • ASSW Business Meetings
  • Arctic Council Senior Arctic Officials Meeting
  • Arctic Observing Summit
  • Model Arctic Council
  • International Arctic Assembly Day, where attendee schedules overlapped. The day was an interactive forum for dialogue on major issues facing Alaska and the Arctic, and concluded with a sold-out banquet.
  • Activities, tours, cultural events and networking opportunities for conference attendees
  • Public lectures, outreach events, workshops, recreational activities, tours and field trips
  • Download the full program (PDF)

Conference statement with recommendations

(23 March 2016) The Arctic is experiencing the fastest rate of climate change and related impacts on the planet. Co-occurring changes are widespread across the biological, physical and human components of the system. These changes are of concern for Arctic peoples and nations and also to the wider global community. For people that call the Arctic home the changes affect livelihoods, economies and cultures, including the ability to respond and plan for the future ...  See the full statement on the Arctic Observing Summit website
